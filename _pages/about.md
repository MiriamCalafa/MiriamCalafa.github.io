Hi, I’m Miriam, a Robotics and Computer Engineering student at the University of Tartu, soon to graduate, with a B.Sc. in Automation Engineering from Politecnico di Milano. My expertise spans across robotics, software development, Human-Robot Interaction (HRI), and system integration, with a strong focus on innovative, open-source technologies.

I am driven by curiosity and a passion for solving complex problems through technical and creative solutions. In my academic and professional journey, I have gained hands-on experience with ROS2, Python, C++, and system integration, contributing to projects in robotic prototyping, R&D, and software development. My thesis focuses on developing a digital twin for a robotic study companion with multimodal emotional expressivity, aimed at enhancing human-robot interactions.

Beyond engineering, I am deeply involved in project management, leadership, and community engagement, having collaborated on cross-functional teams and led educational initiatives. As a Python tutor and teaching assistant, I’ve honed my ability to effectively communicate complex technical concepts to peers, ensuring clarity and fostering a collaborative learning environment. Additionally, I have taken multiple courses on leadership, communication, and interpersonal skills, further refining my ability to engage with diverse teams, lead effectively, and navigate challenging discussions with empathy and tact.

Outside of my professional interests, I am a long-distance runner, committed to discipline, resilience, and continuous improvement—values that guide both my personal and professional life.
